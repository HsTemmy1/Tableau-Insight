INSIGHT

Key Metrics

  • Total Orders: 10K | Total Profit: $286K | Total Quantity Sold: 38K | Total Sales: $2M

Regional Performance

  • West leads in both profit ($108K) and revenue ($725K), followed closely by the East. South and Central regions underperform, signaling opportunities for market development and operational optimization.

Product-Level Insights

  • Phones and Chairs are top revenue drivers, while Fasteners, Labels, and Envelopes underperform.
  • Technology is the most profitable category ($145K), despite Furniture generating significant revenue ($742K) with comparatively low profit ($18K), indicating margin issues.

Customer Segments

  • The Consumer segment accounts for the majority of sales (58%), with Corporate and Home Office contributing 36% and 21%, respectively. There’s potential for growth in the Corporate segment through targeted initiatives.

Geographic Trends

  • Sales are concentrated on the East and West coasts, with Midwest and South regions showing lower engagement—suggesting untapped market potential.

Sales Trends (2014–2018)

  • Monthly sales range from $14K to $118K, with noticeable seasonality and a positive upward trend in 2018. Mid-year dips indicate potential timing or external challenges worth investigating.

Strategic Takeaways

  • Strengths: Technology category, West/East regional dominance, and strong Consumer sales.
  • Challenges: Low Furniture margins and underperformance in South and Central regions.
  • Opportunities: Expand into underperforming regions, improve Furniture profitability, and capitalize on high-margin sub-categories.

Recommendation

  • Deep dive into Furniture cost structures, assess regional marketing strategies, and explore root causes of seasonal sales dips to support sustained growth.
